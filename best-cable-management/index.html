Quick answer

The BlueLounge CableBox is the best all-around cable management solution for most desks: it hides power strips and adapters in a clean, forgiving box that works on floors or tabletops.

If wall-mounted or floor-level cable runs are your main problem, the D-Line Cord Cover delivers a painted, low-profile track; for a premium under-desk system, the UPLIFT Tray keeps everything out of sight but still accessible.

Cable clutter isn’t just an eyesore; it makes unplugging and reconfiguring gear genuinely frustrating. A good cable management solution corrals the mess without making you fight for every plug. The challenge is that no single product works for every desk layout, every cable length, or every budget. Some desks have solid wood tops that can’t clamp; others have open legs perfect for raceways. Some people just need to bundle long power cords behind a TV console; others need a full under-desk tray for multiple power adapters. There are four basic approaches to consider: a tray (for under-desk, out-of-sight storage), a box (for floor or desktop, capturing the bulk), a raceway (for routing cables along walls or desk legs), and simple ties or clips (for bundling and tacking). The right choice depends on where your cables congregate, how often you need to access them, and whether you’re hiding a power strip or just taming a few stray cords.
